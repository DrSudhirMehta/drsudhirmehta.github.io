Culture Doesn’t Crack — It Erodes

The first 10 hires, the co-founder relationship, the equity split, the leadership dynamic… None of these break overnight. They break slowly — through unspoken assumptions, blurred roles, and misaligned expectations.

That’s how company culture erodes. Not through one big failure, but through a thousand small ones.

Like the Roman Colosseum, most cultures don’t collapse in a single dramatic moment. They wear down — slowly, silently — through misalignment, mixed signals, and unresolved tension.

In Beyond the Garage, I keep coming back to one core idea: Clarity isn’t optional. It’s oxygen. Especially when the pressure hits.

Build Trust That Lasts

Most founders obsess over what to build. But the best ones also obsess over who they’re building it with.

Early team breakdowns aren’t caused by bad ideas or weak markets — they’re caused by mismatched personalities, unspoken assumptions, and trust that erodes under pressure.

That’s why I created tools like the Co-Founder Fit Quiz and the Conflict Resolution Worksheet — because building something that lasts starts with building trust that lasts.

Ask yourself:

  • Do we have clarity on how decisions get made?
  • Do we know how we’ll resolve disagreements when — not if — they arise?
  • Do we trust each other enough to be honest?

Beyond the Garage isn’t just about startup success. It’s about startup survivability.
